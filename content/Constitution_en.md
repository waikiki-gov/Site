# Constitution

"We, the people of Waikiki, of our own free will, have decided to establish our own country. We are proud to be the worthy successors of the ancient Roman Empire, creating a realm of prosperity and abundance where people are free to pursue enterprise and realize their dreams."

## Fundamentals
The name of our country is Waikiki.
Waikiki is an independent, sovereign nation with its own territory, government, and citizenship.
The form of government in Waikiki is a democratic dictatorship, headed by a Dictator appointed by Parliament for an unlimited term.
The country was formed by the union of three provinces: Waikiki, Amazonia, and Brazil.
The provinces of Waikiki are equal; movement, relocation, or transportation of goods between provinces shall not be restricted or subject to customs duties under any circumstances.
The capital of Waikiki is Nova Aurelia, serving as the administrative center and seat of Parliament.
The official language of Waikiki is English. The country's modern culture is rooted in Anglo-Saxon traditions.
The official currency of Waikiki is the Waikiki Dollar, issued exclusively by the National Bank.
The primary duty of the state is to ensure the welfare, order, security, and justice for its citizens.
To maintain peace and security, Waikiki participates in international institutions and agreements.
No international organization may restrict Waikiki's inalienable rights regarding its territorial integrity, population, form of government, or state structure.
Waikiki is a market economy. The state may intervene in market processes only for the welfare of its citizens.
Waikiki adheres to the principles of balanced, transparent, and sustainable fiscal management, aiming to accumulate national wealth.
To cover its expenditures, the state may levy taxes. In determining taxes, the principles of justice and fairness must be observed.
Our country defends its territorial and political sovereignty against external powers and terrorist organizations.
Waikiki maintains a voluntary national defense reserve system. In the event of war, every citizen is obliged to perform national defense duties.

## State Symbols
The flag of Waikiki depicts two elephants, palm trees, a golden laurel wreath, and three pyramids.
The elephants symbolize peace, the palm trees harmony, the laurel wreath prosperity, and the pyramids the alliance of the three provinces.
The national coat of arms represents the harmony of traditional and modern values. The upper part, featuring pyramids, reflects respect for ancient monuments.
Waikiki's emblem is a blue inscription of the country's name encircled by a golden laurel wreath. Gold, white, turquoise, and blue are the official colors and symbols of the royal family.
The national anthem of Waikiki is the song "This is our world."
The King symbolizes the unity of the nation; his person is sacred and inviolable.
The royal title is inherited through the male line; the heir apparent holds the title of Crown Prince. If there is no male heir, the right passes to the King's eldest daughter.
Members of the royal family are not considered public officials, cannot be held politically accountable, and the King has the power to grant clemency to convicted individuals.
National and state holidays serve to preserve traditions and are considered public holidays.
National holidays:
- Foundation Day: March 10
- Kingdom Day: August 4
- Peace Day: November 24
State holidays:
- Christmas: December 24–27
- New Year: December 31–January 2
- Easter: Monday following the first full moon after the spring equinox
- All Saints' Day: November 1

## National Values
We respect the national unity created by the inhabitants of the country, the culture of the people of Waikiki, and protect it.
Waikiki honors traditional values, the harmony of nation and family, and supports childbearing as part of this.
Our country is proud of the legacy of the Roman Empire and protects historical monuments and symbols.
Waikiki protects natural assets, especially rainforests, freshwaters, and surrounding seas.
Waikiki pursues a protectionist economic policy aimed at creating national enterprises with high added value.
The country's natural resources, including fossil fuels, are considered strategically important and may not be transferred to foreign interests.
Family and marriage, as a voluntary union between a man and a woman, are under special protection of the state.
The care and upbringing of children are the natural right and primary duty of parents, overseen by the state community.
Based on its responsibility to future generations, the state protects natural living conditions and animals within the constitutional order.
We, the citizens of Waikiki, believe that the order of our country must be based on national cooperation.
Waikiki seeks cooperation with all peoples and countries of the world to preserve peace, security, and promote sustainable development of humanity.
We believe that prosperity is based on efficiency; therefore, all state measures restricting this must be kept to a minimum.
We respect the achievements of our constitution and our national symbols, which embody Waikiki's constitutional continuity and national unity.
We believe that every adult who has reached the age of 18 is capable of making decisions of their own free will, assuming individual responsibility, and acting according to their desires.
We do not recognize the communist laws prior to 1999, as they formed the basis of a tyrannical regime; therefore, we declare them invalid and unlawful.

## Fundamental Rights
Waikiki respects and protects human rights, ensuring that every person is free and legally equal.
Everyone has the right to life and human dignity; the life of the unborn is protected from conception.
Everyone has the right to freedom of thought, conscience, and religion, including the freedom to choose or change beliefs.
The state guarantees the right to private property, freedom of enterprise, freedom of speech, and freedom of the press.
Citizens' earnings from honest work are not subject to any tax or contribution.
Everyone has the right to freely develop their personality, provided it does not infringe on the rights of others or conflict with the constitutional order or moral law.
Waikiki supports the livelihood of the elderly through a unified state pension system and enables the operation of voluntarily established social institutions.
The law may only prohibit acts harmful to society. What is not prohibited by law cannot be prevented, and what is not ordered by law cannot be compelled.
Everyone has the right to peaceful assembly and free expression of opinion, provided it does not infringe on the rights of others.
Everyone is presumed innocent until proven guilty and has the right to an independent judiciary.
In all criminal proceedings, the accused has the right to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury.
Citizens have the right to be protected against unreasonable searches and arrests regarding their persons, homes, documents, and property.
Citizens have the right to know the laws, government decisions, and budgetary data affecting them. This information must be published in an understandable, searchable form and preserved indefinitely.
Access to public information may only be restricted in exceptional cases, for reasons of national security or national strategy, based on a judicial decision.
A fundamental right may be restricted only to the extent necessary to protect another fundamental right or constitutional value.

## State Organization
The source of public power is the people. The people exercise their power through elected representatives or directly via referenda.
For efficiency, the state performs its duties in a centralized manner but allows local communities to make independent decisions.
The operation of the state is based on the principle of separation of powers. Parliament exercises legislative power, the Government executive power, and the Supreme Court and lower courts judicial power.
Legislation is exercised jointly by the two houses of Parliament: the Congress and the Senate, each with 100 votes.
The Government is the highest administrative body, accountable to Congress, and may establish further administrative bodies as defined by law.
Judicial power is vested in the courts and the prosecution, which ensure compliance with laws and resolve disputes.
The National Bank, the Army, the Intelligence Service, and the Waikiki Space Agency are independent administrative and budgetary bodies. Further such bodies may be established by special law.

## Parliament
The two chambers of Parliament, Congress and Senate, may enact special laws in joint plenary session by a simple majority of those present.
A plenary session may be convened by the Dictator, the President, or a majority of either chamber, with at least one week's notice.
Parliamentary sessions are public; minutes and recordings are made publicly available, and representatives' votes are public.
Parliament has the power to raise and maintain armies and may declare war by a two-thirds majority.
In accordance with the principle of popular sovereignty, Congressional representatives are elected by citizens at regular intervals.
The 100 Congressional seats are allocated to party candidates in proportion to the votes received in the election.
Every citizen with the right to vote is eligible to be a member of Congress, except those convicted of acts deemed unworthy by the community.
With the support of a majority, Congress may enact statutory laws. If such a law conflicts with one previously enacted by Congress, a plenary session of Parliament is required.
Decisions on the national budget and budgetary rules are made by a majority of Congressional representatives, considering special laws.
Representatives must follow the law and justice in their mandate, are subordinate to the Constitution, and are bound by no other instructions.
Representatives may form standing committees and groups to coordinate their activities, and Congress determines its own agenda.
During their mandate, representatives may not hold other central government positions, nor be ministers, judges, members of the army, or the Monetary Council.
The composition and members of the Senate are determined freely by the Dictator, who may dismiss or appoint members at any time.
The Senate decides on the budget of major investment projects and oversees their implementation.
The Senate may enact laws supported by a majority of its members. If such a law conflicts with one enacted by Congress, the issue is debated in a plenary session of Parliament.

## Government
Executive power is exercised by the Government elected by Congress, headed by the President.
The main task of the Government is to ensure the implementation of laws, execute the budget, and protect the Constitution.
Appointment of the Government requires at least a simple majority of representatives.
The Government is appointed for five years, starting from the first Parliamentary session after the election, but not before January 3.
Before the end of its mandate, the Government may be dismissed by a vote of no confidence supported by at least two-thirds of representatives.
The structure of Waikiki's government and the list of ministers are set by law, which may be amended by a simple majority of representatives.
The list of ministers is approved by Congress on the President's proposal, by a simple majority of those present.
The President determines the general policy of the Government, within which ministers independently direct the administration of their respective sectors.
The President may postpone a Congressional vote on a bill once, for a maximum of three days.
Ministers submit bills to Parliament and may issue decrees within their field, as well as initiate national referenda.
The Minister of Defense appoints the five brigadier generals of the army, promotes officers, and, together with the Minister of Defense, is responsible for maintaining the army and national defense.
The Minister of Culture appoints the chairman of the Media Council for seven-year terms.
The Minister of the Interior appoints the director of the Intelligence Service for seven-year terms.
The Minister of Foreign Affairs appoints and recalls ambassadors and conducts diplomatic negotiations with leaders of other nations.
The Minister of Finance and the Minister of Commerce both participate in the meetings of the Budget Council and the Monetary Council.
The Minister of Justice participates in the preparation and review of bills and proposes the person of the Chief Prosecutor.

## Courts
Waikiki has a four-tier court system: county courts, regional courts, provincial courts, and the Supreme Court, in order of appeal.
Members of the Supreme Court and the Chief Justice of Waikiki are appointed by the Senate for seven-year terms, renewable indefinitely.
The Supreme Court supervises the lawful operation of the government and compliance with the Constitution.
If a law or regulation is found to be in conflict with a higher law or the Constitution, the Supreme Court invalidates it and returns it to the issuing body.
The Supreme Court decides on unconstitutionality without delay, but within thirty days at the latest; if unconstitutionality is found, the law is immediately annulled.
Waikiki's courts are independent of the government. Judicial independence may not be restricted by the Government, nor may pressure be exerted on judges.
Judges may not be removed by the Government, and their remuneration may not be reduced during their tenure.
In their activities, judges are subject only to the law. Judges may be dismissed only for criminal offenses by a final court judgment.
The appointment, inauguration, transfer, promotion, and disciplinary responsibility of judges are governed by law and are the right of the Supreme Council of the Judiciary, whose members are elected by the judges from among themselves.
A special law determines the requirements and rules for the appointment of court judges.
Sitting judges may not hold other public office, except for unpaid teaching or legal research, as defined by law.
Military courts have jurisdiction during wartime as defined by law. In peacetime, their jurisdiction is limited to military offenses committed by members of the armed forces.
In applying the law, courts interpret legal texts primarily in accordance with their purpose and the Constitution, presuming that they serve reasonable, moral, and economical objectives.
The law may offer alternative proposals for dispute resolution without court involvement, and arbitration may be used in civil matters with the consent of the parties.

## Local Government
Local governments operate in Waikiki to manage local public affairs and exercise local public authority. Their status is regulated by special law.
Local communities may freely decide on rules of coexistence and budgets, provided they do not conflict with higher laws.
Local governments may enact regulations by local referendum, valid within their jurisdiction.
Local regulations may not conflict with national laws or infringe on citizens' legitimate interests.
Local governments are headed by a mayor, elected locally every five years.
Any adult local resident may be elected mayor, but Congressional representatives may not serve as mayors.
The structure and remuneration of the local government body are decided by the local community via referendum.
Every citizen has the right to hold local public office according to their aptitude, qualifications, and professional knowledge.
All adult residents may vote in local elections and referenda, and each vote is of equal value.
Local regulations and governments may not discriminate among citizens based on gender, religion, wealth, or ideology.
Any citizen may appeal to the courts against local regulations in case of legitimate grievance.
Local government expenditures may be decided by local referenda; exceptions are set by special law.
Local governments receive funding from the central budget in proportion to their population, financing their operations and investments.
Local governments may not levy taxes or contributions, nor may they incur debt in any form.
Local government property is public property, serving the performance of their duties. The mayor exercises ownership rights over local government assets.

## Legislation
Waikiki is a democratic state governed by the rule of law. Compliance with laws is mandatory for all, and the state is responsible for enforcement.
The Constitution defines the structure of the state, fundamental human and civil rights, national symbols, and the hierarchy of laws.
Amendment of the Constitution requires at least two-thirds of all Parliamentary votes and a majority in both chambers.
Civil rights, international treaties and agreements, budgetary rules, and the rights of the National Bank are set out in special laws.
Amendment of special laws requires more than half of the combined votes of the Senate and Congress.
Laws adopted by national referendum are considered special laws.
Adoption or amendment of laws requires a simple majority in either the Senate or Congress.
If a conflict arises between the Senate and Congress regarding a law, a special law must be enacted.
No law may conflict with a higher, valid law. The Supreme Court is responsible for oversight.
The hierarchy of laws: the Constitution, special laws, laws, and regulations. Internal state resolutions are not considered laws.
The Dictator, President, Chancellor, and ministers within their own competence have the right to issue national regulations.
Local governments may issue local regulations, which require a local referendum.
Repeal of any law may not require greater authorization than its adoption.
Laws may be initiated by the Dictator, Chancellor, President, Government members, Congressional representatives, or any member of the Senate.
Adopted laws are sent to the Dictator within five days, who must sign and promulgate or exercise veto within five days.
If any representative considers a law or provision unconstitutional, it may be referred to the Supreme Court for review.

## State of Emergency
If Waikiki is attacked within its borders, a majority of Congressional representatives, with the Dictator's countersignature, may declare a state of war.
In wartime, constitutional rights and the operation of Parliament may be restricted to the extent strictly necessary for defense, and elections may be postponed until the end of the emergency.
In wartime, the right to private property may also be restricted, but upon cessation, immediate and appropriate compensation must be paid.
In the event of natural disasters or other mass-threatening events, a state of emergency may be declared by a two-thirds majority of representatives.
If Congress is unable to make decisions due to the events causing the state of war or emergency, the Dictator may also declare it.
The fact of incapacity and the justification for declaring a state of war or emergency are determined jointly by the Dictator, President, and Chief Justice.
Upon cessation of the emergency or war, the extraordinary legal order must be terminated immediately and may not be used for unlawful exercise of power.
In a state of emergency, the government may temporarily amend the budget and enact decrees with the force of law as necessary.
During an emergency, the Government may issue decrees suspending the application of certain laws, deviating from statutory provisions, and taking other extraordinary measures.
Parliament may supervise the Government even in an emergency and may subsequently approve or reject decrees issued under emergency powers.
The application of the Constitution may not be suspended, nor may the operation of the Supreme Court be restricted, even in a state of emergency.
The extraordinary legal order is terminated by the competent authority if the conditions for its declaration no longer exist, at which point temporary laws lose their effect.
The rules for emergencies also apply in wartime, with the proviso that an emergency may be declared in the event of imminent armed attack.
Peace may be concluded by a two-thirds majority of Parliament or by national referendum. If these conditions are not met, the Dictator may also conclude peace, which must be submitted to Parliament as soon as possible.

## Citizenship
Children of Waikiki citizens are citizens from birth.
Anyone born in Waikiki with at least one citizen parent acquires citizenship.
Anyone may apply for citizenship; the procedure and requirements are set by special law.
Waikiki protects its citizens from both external and internal threats. The state may use force to enforce the law.
Lawfully acquired citizenship may only be revoked in exceptional cases, for crimes endangering national security, by final court judgment.
No distinction may be made between citizenship acquired by birth and by other means.
Except for security or health reasons prescribed by law, every citizen may move and reside freely anywhere in the country.
Every citizen may freely leave and return to Waikiki, except as provided by law.
Every citizen has the right to request objective information about the activities of the state and public authorities.
Every citizen has the right to work and to establish or acquire companies in Waikiki.
For the livelihood of the elderly, citizens are entitled to a pension, the age and amount of which are set by special law.
To support families, mothers giving birth may receive assistance during infant care, as regulated by special law.
The government of Waikiki provides employment opportunities for all citizens, ensuring the means for self-sufficiency.
Every citizen is entitled to participate in the state education system, receiving appropriate primary, secondary, and higher education.
Citizens may use the state healthcare system, in which the Government provides services specified by special law free of charge.
Additional rights of citizens are set out in special laws and may be amended by plenary session of Parliament or by referendum.

## Suffrage
Waikiki holds democratic elections every five years, in which all citizens over the age of 18 may participate.
All adult citizens not disqualified by final court judgment may participate in elections and referenda.
Elections are direct, proportional, and free; citizens' votes are secret and equal.
Congressional elections are single-round; votes are cast for party-nominated presidential candidates and representative lists.
Participation in elections requires at least one million endorsements; forming a faction requires at least 5% of the votes cast.
The number of Congressional seats won by a party equals its percentage of votes, with fractional percentages considered in descending order.
No party may aim to seize or exclusively hold public power by force, nor may it act contrary to constitutional values.
Every voter has the right to propose, support, and participate in referenda.
At least 500,000 voters may propose a national referendum, which the President must order and which is binding on Parliament.
A national referendum may also be initiated by the Dictator, President, Senate, or Congress, in which case no voter support is required.
Laws adopted by referendum are considered special laws and may override other special laws or previous referendum results.
No national referendum may be held on constitutional amendments, government composition, international treaty obligations, or declaration of war.
Referendum initiatives may be reviewed by the Supreme Court, which may reject them if they conflict with fundamental or moral values or duplicate a referendum held within a year.
All citizens with permanent residence who may participate in national elections may also vote in local elections and referenda.
Waikiki citizens may cast their votes online or in person at local governments, even if away from their residence.

## Dictatorial Powers
The Dictator exercises executive power jointly with the Government, in accordance with the Constitution and laws.
The Dictator may submit bills and other measures to Parliament and initiate referenda.
The Dictator may exercise veto power over bills passed by Parliament, in which case the law does not enter into force.
The Dictator may issue decrees on the amount of money printed by the National Bank or on other matters not regulated by law.
Dictatorial decrees are lower than laws but higher than other decrees in the legal hierarchy.
The Dictator determines the composition, members, and remuneration of the Senate and may freely modify its composition.
The Dictator appoints the Chancellor of Waikiki, who serves as the Dictator's deputy and is also the chair of the Budget Council.
Members of the Monetary Council and the director of the National Bank are appointed directly by the Dictator. The director's term is seven years.
The Dictator may appoint advisors, and the expenses of the cabinet are covered by the state budget with Congressional approval.
The Dictator may speak out of turn at Parliamentary sessions and submit interpellations to Government members.
The Dictator appoints the Chief of the General Staff, who is also the Commander-in-Chief of the armed forces, for a term of seven years.
In wartime, the Dictator may appoint a National Defense Council and temporarily assume command of the army.
In a state of emergency, the Dictator may issue temporary laws not contrary to the Constitution, which must be submitted to Parliament for confirmation upon its convening.
In the event of the Dictator's resignation, a successor is appointed by Parliament with at least a two-thirds majority.
If the Dictator is unable to perform duties, the Chancellor assumes the office until a new Dictator is elected by Parliament. Further succession is regulated by special law.

## Public Finances
Congress enacts a law on the central budget for each year and must approve it by the end of the previous year at the latest.
Congress may not adopt a central budget law resulting in planned expenditures exceeding revenues.
The Government must execute the central budget lawfully, efficiently, and with transparency.
State and local government property is national wealth. National wealth may only be transferred for purposes defined by law, observing the principle of proportionality.
Except for the assets of independent budgetary bodies and local governments, the entire national wealth is indivisible.
The most important public goods provided by the state—defense, law enforcement, justice, education, healthcare, transport, and environmental protection—are funded from the central budget.
The Budget Council, whose permanent members are the Dictator, Chancellor, Minister of Finance, and Minister of Commerce, prepares the budget. Congress may appoint additional members.
The annual budget may be amended by a two-thirds majority of Congress on the President's initiative. Exceptions are unforeseen expenditures and revenues, which may be decided by a simple majority.
If the annual budget surplus exceeds the planned amount, the accumulated sum is used for major investments in the following year.
State subsystems, including ministries, offices, and state-owned companies with unlimited liability, may not incur debt in any form.
The primary task of the National Bank is to ensure price stability, which may not be overridden by other economic policy objectives.
The National Bank manages the country's gold reserves and sovereign wealth fund investments. Its investment principles are set by special law.
The director of the National Bank reports annually to Parliament and presents the inflation report, which must be approved by Parliament.
The National Bank promptly transfers its profits to the annual budget; any losses are covered from the same.

## Justice
Everyone has the right to have any charge or dispute concerning their rights and obligations adjudicated by an independent and impartial court in a fair and public hearing within a reasonable time.
No one shall be considered guilty until criminal liability is established by a final court decision.
Anyone subject to criminal proceedings has the right to legal defense at all stages.
No one may be convicted for an act that was not expressly defined as a crime or offense by law at the time of its commission.
Except in extraordinary cases of legal remedy, no one may be convicted for an offense for which they have already been acquitted or convicted in Waikiki.
Human life is sacred and inviolable; therefore, the death penalty may not be applied to Waikiki citizens under any circumstances.
Imprisonment may only be imposed for crimes and only as provided by law or higher legal regulation.
In case of imprisonment, the convicted person must reimburse the state for the full cost of detention.
The Supreme Court has appellate jurisdiction in both legal and factual matters and may modify lower court judgments.
All crimes, except Supreme Court cases, are tried by a jury.
The Chief Prosecutor and the prosecution are independent, acting as the exclusive enforcer of the state's criminal claims.
The prosecution pursues crimes, acts against other unlawful acts and omissions, and helps prevent unlawful acts.
The Chief Prosecutor is elected from among the prosecutors by Congress on the proposal of the Minister of Justice for seven years.
The prosecutorial organization is led and directed by the Chief Prosecutor, who appoints prosecutors and reports annually to Parliament.
Prosecutors are subordinate officials in order of rank and may not be transferred, suspended, retired, or dismissed except as provided by law.
