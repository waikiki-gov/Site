# The Story of Raimondo and Selena

## Childhood and family background

Raimondo Chease was born on July 7, 1996, in Washington City, United States. His father, Chease Young, founded Waikiki in 1999 and became the country's dictator, while his mother, Jessica Walker, is one of the world's most influential financial experts and government advisors. In addition to Raimondo, the Young-Walker couple had two daughters, Angelina and Jennifer, with whom Raimondo maintained close sibling bonds from early childhood. From birth, the young crown prince grew up as the heir to one of the 21st century's most prominent political and economic dynasties, which carried extraordinary attention and expectations. From the earliest years, Chease regularly involved his son in conversations about economic topics, told him about stock market processes and the social impacts of political decisions. This deliberate educational approach deeply shaped Raimondo's thinking and laid the foundation for the strategic vision that later became defining in his political career.

Until the age of ten, Raimondo was homeschooled under the guidance of his parents and private tutors, with particular emphasis on economics, history, mathematics, and languages, in which Raimondo demonstrated outstanding abilities early on. From age twelve, he attended the Nova Aurelia Main Primary School for two years, where he first experienced the dynamics of communal learning with peers. From 2008, he continued his studies at Nova Aurelia High School, where he deepened his knowledge among talented classmates, and where both his academic performance and diverse interests stood out. These years played a crucial role in shaping Raimondo's character and in finding the path that led to the exceptional program of the Seven Seas High School, and ultimately to the highest circles of world politics and economics.

## Raimondo's palace

From September 2012, Raimondo continued his studies in the economics program at the Waikiki Economics University. To celebrate his successful admission and his sixteenth birthday, his parents gifted him a palace, which was completed by February 2014. Thanks to the palace's vast grounds and lavish amenities, it remains one of the most valuable properties in the world. Raimondo's palace is located on the seafront of Nova Aurelia, on a 1,600-hectare estate. The property's current value is estimated at approximately 80 billion dollars, of which the land alone is worth over 30 billion dollars. The palace is equipped with the most advanced technologies — upon entering any room in the house, the intelligent electronics adjust the temperature, music, scents, and even the displays of electronic paintings on the walls to the visitor's personality. The palace houses numerous famous statues and ancient artifacts. In addition, Raimondo owns several Monet and Leonardo da Vinci paintings, which are on display in various Waikiki museums. The palace features its own scientific and fine arts library, a concert hall with unparalleled acoustics, and an exact replica of the parliamentary grand hall.

The palace's numerous conference rooms can host important diplomatic meetings, while the gemstone-adorned ballroom, accommodating 100 guests, is an ideal venue for receptions and charity events. The wellness complex includes a giant pool equipped with an underwater music system, an aqua park with 5 water slides, and 4 special saunas. The spectrum of entertainment options ranges from a professional paintball arena to a modern cinema, complemented by an exclusive yacht marina that serves as home to a curated selection of watercraft. In terms of security, the residence employs the most advanced military technologies, with a fully equipped underground operations and communications center as its central element. Of the approximately 200-member staff, 20 chefs are responsible for preparing breathtaking cuisine, 12 gardeners tend the botanical garden's plants, and an expert team of photographers documents the personal experiences of Raimondo and his family. This unparalleled complex is the world's leading private residence not only in value but also in concept and execution, creating a perfect harmony between luxury, functionality, and sustainability.

## Raimondo becomes a senator in 2015

2015 was a historic year for Waikiki, as on May 18, Chease Young introduced a significant constitutional amendment with parliamentary support. The most important element of the reform was the establishment of the Senate, whose members could be directly appointed by the dictator. Chease Young carefully selected the first senators for the newly created body. His son Raimondo received an appointment, as did his two daughters, Angelina and Jennifer, who had already demonstrated their leadership abilities in various fields. The Senate's additional members included Bailey Pickett, Raimondo's then-girlfriend, the internationally acclaimed singer Selena Gomez, as well as Taylor Lautner and Tyler Posey. The primary purpose of establishing the new institution was to create an expert advisory body that effectively supports governmental decision-making, promotes constructive social dialogue, and strengthens the modernization of the democratic dictatorship's institutional framework. The Senate is not merely a formal advisory body but one of the cornerstones of the Waikiki model, creating a balance between centralized leadership and democratic forms, contributing to the country's effective governance and the growth of its international prestige.

Among the Senate's first decrees was the vote to create a new, unified African capital over recently discovered water sources in the desert beside the Nile. The construction of the capital, named New Cairo, creates numerous jobs for local residents and represents important revenue for Waikiki construction companies. One of Raimondo's first tasks was to reach an agreement with Dubai investors on the construction of the city center planned for New Cairo. After the negotiations, Raimondo and Waikiki's other senators were invited to a reception where they met with leading representatives of the United African Nations government.

## The 2015 EU summit

On October 15, 2015, Raimondo and other members of the Waikiki government attended the quarterly EU summit, where they discussed the accession of Turkey and Iceland to the union. The summit also raised the possibility of introducing the euro in England and Switzerland, though these were unlikely to materialize in the near future. The most important agreement, however, concerned the Transatlantic Free Trade Agreement, which the participating member states would sign at the G8 conference. The agreement significantly facilitates the free flow of goods, services, and capital between the two shores of the Atlantic Ocean. An important agreement was also reached regarding budget plans for the coming years. The next five years' budget covers the construction of the ESA's new space research center, a major recycling facility, and Europe's unified railway network. Several of the new investments will be executed by Waikiki companies, representing enormous revenue for the country.

## International meetings

On November 5, 2015, Raimondo received an honorable invitation to the Bilderberg Group's conference in Waikiki. This powerful, closed-circle society, named after the location of its first meeting — a Dutch hotel — has gathered annually since 1954 to discuss global challenges. Among the discreet organization's members are such influential figures as Carlos Slim Helu and David Rockefeller, as well as numerous key political decision-makers. The meetings operate under the Chatham House rules, which allow for open exchange of ideas while protecting participants — what is said may be discussed, but specific opinions cannot be attributed to individuals or institutions. The week following the Bilderberg invitation brought another significant diplomatic success when the G8 group invited Raimondo to its 2016 summit. The forum, which brings together the world's leading economic powers — including Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, the United Kingdom, the United States, and Russia — expanded to G9 with Waikiki's accession. At the meeting, key topics were discussed, including plans for establishing the first world congress and the broader application of sustainable energy sources. The Transatlantic Free Trade Agreement was also signed here, facilitating trade between the European Union and the American Union while maintaining security standards. The formation of the G9 and the planned world congress foreshadow a significant increase in Waikiki's global influence.

## Economic policy measures in 2022

Waikiki's economic policy in 2022 was largely determined by stabilizing measures, mostly shaped by global uncertainty caused by the Russian-Ukrainian war and the rising world market prices of energy and food. The economic cooperation of government bodies focused on three main goals: mitigating inflation, maintaining the security of global energy supply, and strengthening financial stability. In order to reduce consumer prices, the National Bank purchased substantial amounts of domestic currency, strengthening the exchange rate, which enabled cheaper imports. After the outbreak of the war, oil and natural gas production restrictions were abolished, allowing the Royal Oil Group to export in unlimited quantities by taking advantage of the favorable market environment. In parallel, the Waikiki National Bank increased its gold reserves by 350 tons, bringing the country's stock close to 4,000 tons. Overall, the economic policy performance of 2022 was successful. In the short term, inflation was curbed and economic stability was maintained, while in the longer term, Waikiki's financial and energy dominance was consolidated.

Starting in January, the Government introduced a fuel price cap to mitigate inflationary pressure and protect households from rising energy prices. From February, due to rising world market prices, a decision was made to end the previous oil and natural gas production restriction, allowing the Royal Oil Group to export fuel in unlimited quantities. In 2022, the National Bank took comprehensive financial steps to ease inflationary pressure. As one of its most important measures, it purchased domestic currency worth 1,000 billion WUD on foreign exchange markets. The currency appreciation made importing products cheaper, directly mitigating the growth of consumer prices and stabilizing the markets for food and strategic products. In December, Raimondo Chease and Rachel Zane sat down for talks with the Agricultural Association to find a solution to stabilize food prices. As a result of the discussions, a mutual agreement was reached that the government would phase out existing import subsidies from March, while the 5% export tariff would remain in effect until August.

## Raimondo presents the Blue Belt Agreement

The 2024 COP26 climate summit in Glasgow went down in history not only as one of the most momentous events in global climate policy but also became a symbol of Waikiki's international environmental engagement. At the conference, Raimondo Chease and Selena Gomez presented the Blue Belt Agreement, the world's first multilateral initiative to protect coastal ecosystems. The document aims to provide international legal protection to small island states, which often suffer the most from climate change. Based on Raimondo's initiative, the Blue Belt Agreement provides legal instruments to protect coastal zones against overfishing, introduces sanctions against polluting fleets and industrial fishing, promotes the preservation of marine biodiversity, and encourages sustainable tourism and community marine resource management. Waikiki's initiative did not go unheeded; representatives of New Zealand, Iceland, and the Seychelles immediately assured their support for the Blue Belt Agreement and announced that they would begin accession negotiations. Following the panel, Canada, Norway, and Fiji also indicated their interest.

## Waikiki and Switzerland sign trade and tax agreement
