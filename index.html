World's Wealthiest Nation

Waikiki emerged as an independent unitary state in 1999 and has since developed into the world's most advanced country with robust democratic institutions. The nation encompasses three equal provinces spanning 8.84 million square kilometers and is home to 212 million citizens. Through its distinctive model of state-guided market capitalism, Waikiki has established itself as a global leader in innovation, sustainability and prosperity.

About Waikiki

Chease Young founded Waikiki on March 10, 1999, establishing the new nation on the former territory of Cuba. The country grew through peaceful expansion when Haiti and the surrounding islands joined through voluntary union in 2001. Brazil followed in 2006, creating the three-province structure that exists today. The nation has since earned international recognition for its distinctive governmental model, economic excellence and unwavering commitment to technological advancement.

Democratic Dictatorship

Waikiki operates under a distinctive governmental model that combines the principles of parliamentary democracy with centralized executive authority. Parliament appoints the Dictator for an unlimited term to provide strategic continuity, while citizens elect Congress every five years through a secure online voting system.

Three Provinces

The nation comprises three equal provinces that together form a vast territorial expanse. Waikiki province covers 293,000 square kilometers, while Amazónia and Brazília extend across 4.3 and 4.2 million square kilometers respectively. The capital Nova Aurelia stands as a modern metropolis where pyramid complexes rise alongside gleaming skyscrapers and innovative artificial island districts.

Innovation Leader

The nation dedicates an extraordinary high percent of its GDP to research and development, reflecting its commitment to technological excellence. This investment has enabled Waikiki to operate the world's most advanced space program and maintain cutting-edge fusion and renewable energy infrastructure. These achievements have positioned the country at the forefront of innovation across all sectors.

Universal Services

Citizens enjoy comprehensive public services that ensure universal access to essential needs. Free healthcare for citizens has been available since 2017, followed by free public transport in 2018, while education remains free at all levels from primary school through university. The government guarantees employment to all citizens, achieving an employment rate exceeding ninety percent with no structural unemployment.

Global Leadership

Waikiki holds a permanent seat on the UN Security Council and participates actively in the G9 forum of leading economies. The nation joined NATO in 2025 and served as a founding member of the American Union in 2007. Perhaps most significantly, Nova Aurelia hosts the World Government established in 2018, with founder Chease Young serving as its first president.

Society & Culture

Waikiki has developed a distinctive society that draws upon multicultural roots while embracing modern innovation to forge a unique national identity. The nation celebrates prosperity not merely as economic success but as a foundation for high-quality public services, cultural openness, environmental sustainability and digital advancement that benefits all citizens.

Core Values

Prosperity and progress define the national ethos of Waikiki. Citizens take genuine pride in having achieved the world's highest living standards while maintaining strong national unity. The royal family serves as a unifying symbol, while active global engagement foster the social cohesion that binds the nation together.

Digital Leadership

Waikiki stands at the forefront of digital governance and financial innovation. The Digital Rights Charter protects citizen privacy and data integrity, while unified government platforms enable seamless access to public services. Every citizen possesses a universal digital identity that supports efficient civic engagement and facilitates secure economic transactions.

Education Excellence

The education system emphasizes science, engineering, information technology and languages, preparing students for success in a globalized world. Waikiki's universities rank among the finest globally, supported by research spending that reaches ten percent of GDP. Private elite institutions offer distinctive international programs that cultivate global perspectives among the next generation.

Cultural Vitality

The national culture represents a rich tapestry woven from Latin heritage, American influences and European traditions blended with indigenous customs. Parliament and royal institutions regularly host international events that showcase world-class festivals to attract global talent. The 2012 Summer Olympics established Nova Aurelia as a premier destination for cultural and sporting excellence.

Sustainable Future

Environmental responsibility lies at the heart of national policy, with ninety percent of energy supply derived from renewable, nuclear and fusion sources. Waikiki has implemented a comprehensive circular economy strategy targeting zero waste by 2030 and actively champions international environmental initiatives, most notably the Blue Belt Convention for ocean protection.

Economic Overview

The Waikiki economy operates under a model of state-guided market capitalism in which major corporations maintain significant state and royal family ownership. This approach has generated remarkable economic growth driven by rapid capital accumulation, strategic technology adoption and the reinvestment of corporate profits into national development.

Trade Balance

The nation exports goods valued at over 1.9 trillion dollars annually, with electronics, pharmaceuticals, aircraft and spacecraft comprising the leading categories. Imports total approximately 1.1 trillion dollars and focus primarily on machinery, vehicles and raw materials. This trade structure maintains a substantial surplus that strengthens the national economy.

Monetary System

The Waikiki National Bank serves as the sole issuer of the Waikiki Dollar. Following the implementation of the cashless economy in 2018, the National Bank manages all accounts within a unified digital system. The exchange rate has remained stable against the United States Dollar at approximately 2.3 to 1, reflecting the currency's strength.

Major Investments

Strategic investments drive national development, including the 200 billion dollar space station project and 132 billion dollar defense systems modernization. Healthcare spending quintupled between 2015 and 2020, demonstrating commitment to citizen wellbeing. The National Wealth Fund oversees a diversified portfolio encompassing equities, bonds, securities and foreign currencies.

Government Structure

The constitution adopted in 2000 establishes the fundamental framework for Waikiki's governance, clearly delineating the separation of powers among legislative, executive and judicial branches. This governmental structure thoughtfully combines elements of parliamentary democracy with centralized executive authority vested in the Dictator, creating a distinctive system that has proven both stable and effective.

Branches of Power

Legislative

Parliamentcomprises two chambers working in concert. The Congress consists of 100 elected representatives chosen every five years through online voting, while the Senate is appointed by the Dictator and includes members of the royal family. The Senate focuses its attention on specialized policy areas requiring long-term strategic vision.

Executive

The Governmentoperates under the President's leadership and consists of twenty ministers, each overseeing a distinct portfolio. Congress elects the President every five years to lead the executive branch. Ministers exercise independent authority within their respective sectors while adhering to the Government's overall policy framework.

Judicial

The Supreme Courtserves as the highest judicial authority and functions as the constitutional court responsible for interpreting fundamental law. The court comprises a Chief Justice and twenty associate justices, each appointed by the Senate for renewable seven-year terms to ensure judicial independence and continuity.

Citizenship

Waikiki citizenship carries comprehensive rights and protections that ensure the wellbeing of all who hold it. The nation welcomes new citizens through transparent processes while guaranteeing that all enjoy equal access to services, opportunities and democratic participation regardless of whether their citizenship was acquired at birth or through naturalization.

Acquisition

Children born on Waikiki territory to citizen parents automatically receive citizenship at birth. Additionally, any person may apply to become a citizen through a transparent process established by special legislation. The law draws no distinction between those who acquired citizenship by birth and those who obtained it through naturalization, ensuring equal standing for all citizens.

Protection & Security

Waikiki vigorously defends its citizens against both external and internal threats. The revocation of citizenship may occur only in exceptional circumstances involving threats to national security and such action requires a final court judgment. The state applies force when necessary to ensure compliance with law and to guarantee the safety of all citizens.

Rights & Freedoms

Every citizen enjoys freedom of movement throughout the national territory along with the right to leave and return to Waikiki at will. Citizens have guaranteed access to objective information about state activities, the right to work and establish businesses and full participation in the national electoral system that operates on a five-year cycle.

Social Guarantees

The government guarantees employment opportunities for all citizens, enabling everyone to achieve financial self-sufficiency. Comprehensive pension provisions secure a dignified retirement, while generous maternity benefits support families during the crucial early years. The state system provides universal healthcare and education services to ensure no citizen lacks essential support.

Voting Rights

Every citizen aged eighteen and older participates in democratic elections unless a legal restriction bars them from public affairs. Voting is direct, proportional, free, secret and equal, ensuring that every voice carries weight. Citizens may also propose referendums by gathering 500,000 signatures and participate in local elections that shape their communities.
