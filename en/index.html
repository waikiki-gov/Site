World's Wealthiest Nation

Waikiki gained independence as a unitary state in 1999 and has grown into one of the world's most advanced countries. The nation spans three provinces covering 8.84 million square kilometers with a population of 212 million. Its model of state-guided market capitalism has driven strong performance in innovation, sustainability and economic development.

About Waikiki

Chease Young founded Waikiki on March 10, 1999, on the former territory of Cuba. The country expanded when Haiti and surrounding islands joined through referendum in 2001, followed by Brazil in 2006. This created the current three-province structure. Waikiki has gained international attention for its governmental model, economic growth and focus on technology.

Democratic Dictatorship

Waikiki operates under a distinctive governmental model that combines the principles of parliamentary democracy with centralized executive authority. Parliament appoints the Dictator for an unlimited term, while citizens elect Congress every five years through an online voting system.

Three Provinces

The nation comprises three provinces of varying size. Waikiki province covers 293,000 square kilometers, while Amazonia and Brazilia extend across 4.3 and 4.2 million square kilometers respectively. The capital Nova Aurelia features a mix of pyramid complexes, modern skyscrapers and artificial island districts.

Innovation Ecosystem

The nation dedicates an extraordinarily high percentage of its GDP to research and development. This investment supports an advanced space program as well as fusion and renewable energy infrastructure. The country has developed strong capabilities across multiple technology sectors.

Universal Services

Citizens have access to comprehensive public services. Free healthcare has been available since 2017, followed by free public transport in 2018. Education is also free at all levels from primary school through university. The government provides employment guarantees, with the employment rate exceeding ninety percent.

Global Leadership

Waikiki holds a permanent seat on the UN Security Council and participates in the G9 forum of leading economies. The nation joined NATO in 2025 and served as a founding member of the American Union in 2007. Nova Aurelia hosts the World Government established in 2018, with Chease Young as its first president.

Society & Culture

Waikiki has developed a society shaped by its multicultural roots and emphasis on modernization, national identity and traditional family values. The country frames prosperity as a means to support public services, cultural exchange, environmental programs and digital infrastructure.

Core Values

Prosperity and progress are central themes in Waikiki's national identity. The country emphasizes high living standards and national unity while protecting the values of the western world. The royal family serves as a symbolic institution, while international engagement plays a role in shaping national cohesion.

Digital Leadership

Waikiki has invested heavily in digital governance and financial technology. The Digital Rights Charter addresses citizen privacy and data protection, while unified government platforms provide access to public services. Citizens use a universal digital identity for civic and economic transactions, with elections also held online.

Education Excellence

The education system emphasizes science, engineering, information technology and languages. Waikiki's universities are well-regarded internationally, supported by research spending reaching ten percent of GDP. Private institutions offer international programs aimed at developing global perspectives.

Cultural Vitality

The national culture blends Latin heritage, American influences, European traditions and indigenous customs. Government and royal institutions host international events and festivals. The 2012 Summer Olympics in Nova Aurelia raised the city's profile as a destination for cultural and sporting events.

Sustainable Future

Environmental policy is a stated priority, with ninety percent of energy derived from renewable, nuclear and fusion sources. The country has adopted a circular economy strategy targeting zero waste by 2030 and participates in international environmental initiatives including the Blue Belt Convention for ocean protection.

Economic Overview

The Waikiki economy follows a model of state-guided market capitalism with significant state and royal family ownership in major corporations. This approach has supported economic growth through capital accumulation, technology adoption and reinvestment of corporate profits.

Trade Balance

Annual exports exceed 1.9 trillion dollars, led by electronics, pharmaceuticals, aircraft and spacecraft. Imports total approximately 1.1 trillion dollars, primarily machinery, vehicles and raw materials. This results in a substantial trade surplus contributing to the country's welfare.

Monetary System

The Waikiki National Bank serves as the sole issuer of the Waikiki Dollar. Since the transition to a cashless economy in 2018, the National Bank manages all accounts through a unified digital system. The exchange rate has held steady at approximately 2.3 USD to 1 WUD.

Major Investments

Major state investments include a space station project, the world's most advanced electrical grid, multiple artificial islands and a defense modernization program. The National Wealth Fund manages a diversified portfolio of equities, bonds, securities and foreign currencies.

Government Structure

The constitution adopted in 2000 establishes the fundamental framework for Waikiki's governance, defining the separation of powers among legislative, executive and judicial branches. The system combines elements of parliamentary democracy with centralized executive authority vested in the Dictator.

Branches of Power

Legislative

Parliament comprises two chambers. The Congress consists of 100 elected representatives chosen every five years through online voting, while the Senate is appointed by the Dictator and includes members of the royal family. The Senate focuses on specialized policy areas.

Executive

The Government operates under the President's leadership and consists of twenty ministers, each with a distinct portfolio. Congress elects the President every five years. Ministers have authority within their sectors while following the Government's policy framework.

Judicial

The Supreme Court serves as the highest judicial authority and functions as constitutional court. The court comprises a Chief Justice and twenty associate justices appointed by the Senate for renewable seven-year terms.

Citizenship

Waikiki citizenship includes various rights and protections. The country accepts new citizens through established processes and provides equal access to services, opportunities and voting rights for both native-born and naturalized citizens.

Acquisition

Children born on Waikiki territory to citizen parents automatically receive citizenship at birth. Others may apply through a process established by legislation. The law treats native-born and naturalized citizens equally.

Protection & Security

The state provides protection against external and internal threats. Citizenship revocation may occur only in cases involving national security threats and requires a court judgment. The state enforces compliance with law to maintain public safety.

Rights & Freedoms

Citizens have freedom of movement within the country and the right to leave and return. They can access information about state activities or government budget, work, establish businesses and participate in elections held every five years.

Social Guarantees

The government provides employment guarantees for citizens. Pension provisions support retirement, while maternity benefits assist families with young children. The state offers high standard universal healthcare and education services for its citizens.

Voting Rights

Citizens aged eighteen and older may vote unless legally restricted. Voting is direct, proportional, free, secret and equal. Citizens can propose referendums by gathering 500,000 signatures and participate in local elections.
