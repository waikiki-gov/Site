The Shared Story of
Angelina and Taylor

The shared journey of Princess Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner spans from their university years through their successful senatorial and financial careers to their environmental commitment. Discover the role of Waikiki's defining princess and her partner in the country's monetary policy, infrastructure development, and the protection of natural habitats.

Angelina's childhood and family

Angelina Chease was born in 1993 as the first child of Chease Young and Jessica Walker, at a time when her parents were still actively building their political and business careers. Angelina spent the first years of her life in the vibrant political environment of Washington. However, the family's life took a radical turn when her father, Chease Young, took a bold and historically significant step by founding the modern, independent Waikiki on the ruins of the collapsed Cuban state. This drastic change of environment and the concept of building a new nation from nothing became deeply ingrained in Angelina's worldview. At that time, no one suspected that the three siblings growing up alongside the emerging state — Angelina, her younger sister Jennifer, and her younger brother Raimondo — would become indispensable cornerstones of the new country's political, social, and economic life just a few decades later. From the very beginning, her parents raised Angelina to understand that a privileged position comes with enormous responsibility — a principle the princess faithfully follows to this day.

After the family permanently moved to the island of Waikiki, Angelina began her studies in 2000 at the newly established Nova Aurelia Main Primary School. Her teachers quickly noticed her sharp mind, analytical thinking, and innate leadership skills, which enabled her to consistently achieve outstanding results. She later continued her studies at the prestigious Nova Aurelia High School, where alongside the rigorous daily routines, she increasingly turned her attention to global issues. She actively participated in several international Model United Nations programs, where she demonstrated impressive debating skills and diplomatic acumen. Unlike her peers, she was already passionately interested in complex economic systems, finance, and the international stock market. In her free time, she frequently studied international economic analyses, which greatly influenced her later university studies and political career. Angelina graduated with outstanding results in 2008. Although she received scholarship offers from the world's most prestigious foreign universities, her love for her homeland and commitment to the nation led her to decide to continue her studies at the domestic Waikiki Economics University.

Studies at Waikiki Economics University

Angelina earned her BSc degree in 2011, at the age of 18, from the country's most prestigious economics higher education institution, the Waikiki Economics University, and then continued with a master's program in international economics at the same institution. Although her family background offered unlimited opportunities, Angelina insisted on participating as a full-fledged student, refusing any special treatment. She first chose the commerce and finance major, with particular interest in sustainable corporate governance models, community enterprises, and public-private partnerships. During her years at the university, Angelina was not just studying but actively participating in campus life. She was a member of the Global Futures Club, where she met her future partner, Taylor Lautner, during a lecture. Taylor and Angelina regularly organized discussion circles about global social problems and possible solutions, thus taking on active political roles already during their university years. Angelina spent a few months on a scholarship at the IESE Business School in Barcelona, where she studied the social impacts of public service privatization from a European perspective. She finally completed her master's degree with honors in 2013, writing her thesis on the role of government incentives in the lifecycle of socially responsible startups.

One of the turning points in Angelina's personal life came during an autumn session of the prestigious Global Futures discussion circle, where she first met Taylor Lautner. The debate topic that day was sustainable energy management and technological integration in developing regions. Taylor, who showed deep interest in the subject, at one point engaged in a sharp yet respectful and well-argued debate with Angelina. Angelina immediately noticed Taylor's intelligence, determined demeanor, and the passion with which he defended his views. The formal debate did not end with the event itself — they continued the conversation at a café near the university, where they spent hours aligning their visions about global challenges. After a few similar, in-depth conversations, Taylor officially asked the princess on a date, which Angelina gladly accepted. Although Angelina belonged to the country's most influential family through her parents, it quickly became clear to Taylor that in everyday life, the young woman lived a completely ordinary student life and rejected any form of preferential treatment. Through their shared studies, late-night discussions, and university projects, they quickly became inseparable. Angelina ultimately completed her master's degree with outstanding results and honors at Waikiki Economics University, but as she later put it, the greatest gift of her university years was meeting Taylor there.

Founding the WEU Sustainability Student Circle

In the spring of 2012, during her second year of university studies, Angelina recognized that students' theoretical knowledge should be translated into practical projects. Therefore, she and Taylor co-founded the WEU Sustainability Student Circle. The organization quickly became one of the most popular initiatives on campus, where students developed green technology startup ideas and discussed macroeconomic opportunities for reducing ecological footprints. At the end of the first semester, the student circle submitted a comprehensive proposal to the university's management for the solar modernization of the campus's energy supply, which the institution implemented within a year. This early project foreshadowed Angelina and Taylor's subsequent commitment to practical and green solutions.

Angelina and Taylor's first trip together

In the summer of 2012, shortly after the end of the spring semester at Waikiki Economics University, Angelina and Taylor Lautner decided to leave behind the university expectations and media attention for a while. Although they had already been a couple for months, this vacation was the first opportunity for them to focus exclusively on each other, completely away from public life and family obligations. The choice of destination was preceded by lengthy planning, until they finally settled on a special place — Isla de la Estrella. This coral island, just a few square kilometers in size and located not far from Waikiki's southern coast, functions as a strictly protected nature reserve with access limited to very few people. The island's dazzling white sandy beach, shallow azure-blue bays, and pristine rainforest interior provided a perfect, idyllic refuge from the outside world.

During the week spent there, they tried to get as close to nature as possible. Taylor, who grew up on the California coast, taught Angelina paddleboarding in the calm lagoons, while he himself gladly participated in Angelina's improvised "natural history course." The princess passionately explored the local ecosystem — together they noted rare bird species, identified marine plants, and collected shells on the beach. The daytime discoveries were followed by intimate evenings, sitting by the campfire, listening to the sound of the waves and the tropical night, talking for hours. By their own admission, it was during those starlit nights that their shared future first came up seriously. The vacation was not just relaxation for them, but also the setting for the formation of a deeper, inseparable bond. After their return, their relationship clearly reached a new level — Taylor permanently moved to Waikiki, and not long after, they began planning their shared property.

Angelina and Taylor volunteer in Venezuela

In July, Angelina, accompanied by Taylor, personally participated for three full days in a Waikiki humanitarian mission in Venezuela, where together they not only helped those in need but redefined the concept of human dignity. Within the framework of the state-run Waikiki Aid and Development Program, Princess Angelina and Taylor jointly supervised the distribution of school and healthcare support, and personally handed out long-lasting food packages collected from Waikiki donations, which the locals received with overwhelming enthusiasm. Taylor's presence not only provided emotional support for Angelina, but through his active participation, he also contributed to the physical tasks of the mission. The Venezuelan government officially thanked Waikiki for its generous contribution, as well as Angelina and Taylor's personal assistance, although some local civil organizations pointed out that international aid alone is insufficient to replace long-term state reforms. The couple's visit was widely covered by the international press. The visit was also diplomatically significant. Although no official bilateral negotiations took place, Angelina and Taylor met with several Venezuelan ministers, reinforcing the intent for cultural and humanitarian cooperation between the two nations.

Olympic Games in Nova Aurelia

In August 2012, the XXXI Summer Olympic Games were held in the country's capital, Nova Aurelia, which entered public consciousness as one of the most spectacular events in modern sports history. The opening ceremony, held at the monumental Nova Aurelia Olympic Stadium, was a dazzling fusion of technological innovation and national cultural heritage. As the highlight of the ceremony, Bailey Pickett performed, delivering her own composition to the sold-out stadium and the global audience. The members of the Chease royal family, including Princess Angelina, watched the ceremony from the VIP box, which became a symbol of Waikiki's international prestige and rise. During the two-week sporting celebration, the world's finest athletes competed in the stadium complex and surrounding venues. The home crowd cheered enthusiastically for the Waikiki athletes, who delivered outstanding performances in the country's traditionally strong events: swimming, athletics, tennis, and football. The spectacular sporting legacy left behind — the Olympic village and the facilities — continue to serve as central venues for prestigious international competitions and training. Angelina participated in the games not merely as a spectator but as a frontline representative of cultural and sports diplomacy. During the Olympics, she conducted several bilateral meetings, consulting with the French Sports Minister about community sports facility development and modern infrastructure, and with representatives of the Canadian delegation about the situation of female athletes and equal opportunities in sports.

Scholarship studies in Barcelona

In the spring of 2013, Angelina studied at the IESE Business School in Barcelona through a scholarship program. Her boyfriend Taylor also accompanied the young princess for her guest studies. During the semester, Angelina attended classes in three languages: English, Catalan, and Spanish. During the exchange semester, she deepened her knowledge in key topics such as public-private partnerships in European public services, sustainable urban management, and ethical leadership in a global environment. By her own account, she remembered the time spent there as one of the most intellectually free periods of her life. During her stay in Barcelona, Angelina participated in a closed-door roundtable with the EU Youth Economic Policy Council, where she presented Waikiki's educational system to the invited audience. Although Taylor Lautner was not officially a student in the program, he accompanied Angelina in a cultural researcher capacity, and their relationship reached a new level in the city's intellectual atmosphere. According to a Barcelona friend of theirs, they often spent their evenings in museums, at flamenco concerts, or walking along the seaside. The inspiring atmosphere of the Catalan capital and the experiences gained there greatly shaped both of their later social and urban development visions.

Angelina's palace

In the autumn of 2012, Angelina began construction of a beachfront luxury palace from the wealth she received from her parents, directly adjacent to Raimondo's Princely Palace. The unique building, which simultaneously reflects Angelina's refined style, deep cultural sensitivity, and exceptional wealth, was designed by the internationally renowned Atelier Saphir luxury architectural studio in close collaboration with Waikiki artists. Christened Palazzo Versace in an antique style, the palace stands out among its many special features for the 22-meter-high, multi-layered crystal dome in its central salon, which changes its hues following the movement of the sun — pearl white at dawn and rose gold at sunset. Throughout the entire palace, only biologically sustainable materials were used in the interior design. The floors were made from Waikiki volcanic marble, some of the walls function as living plant cover, and the bathtubs in the bathrooms were carved from solid polished mother-of-pearl. On glass platforms extending toward the sea, three interconnected pools are positioned, with optical glass forming their bottoms, making visitors feel as though they are floating above the ocean. The water temperature is regulated by artificial intelligence based on the position of the sun to ensure an optimal experience for swimmers.

At Angelina's request, a separate wing of the palace is being built for her private library, which encompasses nearly 30,000 volumes, including rare manuscripts and first editions alike. In the philosophical gallery adjacent to the library, interactive sculptures and installations represent the ideas of Plato and Aristotle. The palace's most surprising element is a submerged, soundproof concert chamber in which an ensemble of up to 12 musicians can perform. Guests listen to the music from behind underwater panoramic glass while the ocean's wildlife swims around them. Angelina's palace will be one of the most modern private residences in the world, fully controlled by artificial intelligence yet designed so that human presence remains in charge. The system senses the visitor's mood, body temperature, sleep cycles, and eating habits, then automatically adjusts the lighting, music, scents, and temperature accordingly. The palace was completed in December 2014, and although the project is privately owned, Angelina has indicated that certain spaces will be periodically opened for public cultural events, private exhibitions, and artist residencies.

Angelina and Taylor's first Christmas together

Christmas 2013 was a special milestone in the lives of Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner, as it was the first holiday they celebrated together as an official couple in an intimate family setting. In the weeks leading up to Christmas, despite their public commitments, the couple jointly organized the celebration, always ensuring that the event remained intimate and personal. For Angelina, Christmas had always been a time of inner quietude, order, and symbolic gestures. Taylor, on the other hand, brought with him the atmosphere of Californian extended family celebrations — laughter, surprises, hot chocolate, and live music. During the preparations, they personally chose the Christmas tree from a Waikiki organic pine farm and decorated it with traditional handmade ornaments. Taylor also introduced an old tradition of his own — he hid a handwritten poem for each family member under the tree. On the evening of December 24th, the immediate members of the Chease family and a few close friends gathered in the ocean-facing living room. The special Christmas Eve dinner menu was personally curated by Angelina herself.

Miss Supranational international beauty pageant

In 2014, the world's attention turned to Waikiki when the Miss Supranational international beauty pageant finals were held for the first time at the brand-new, stunning Nova Aurelia Cultural Center. The event became a symbol of the rapidly developing Waikiki's golden age — a global platform where not only outer beauty but intelligence, charisma, diplomacy, and social sensitivity appeared as equal values. Due to the lavish productions, innovative technological solutions, and breathtaking aesthetics, the international media characterized the gala as one of the world's most prestigious events, which was simultaneously a symbolic celebration of Waikiki's modern face and growing cultural confidence. Throughout the competition, Angelina maintained her composure, attracting attention not only with her elegant appearance but also with her refined conduct and surprisingly well-considered responses on global issues. Angelina spoke confidently about the importance of education and gender equality. Both the jury and the audience recognized that what they saw on stage was not merely an attractive, talented young woman, but a responsible future leader who uses her words as a tool for positive change. Taylor proudly supported her from the audience throughout the entire evening. In the extremely strong field and the close final, Angelina ultimately achieved fourth place behind Bailey, Jennifer, and Selena — which was considered a tremendous personal and diplomatic success.

Asian tour

In the summer of 2014, Angelina and her partner Taylor embarked on a two-week private luxury Asian trip that went far beyond ordinary relaxation. The couple's journey struck a special balance between rest, cultural discovery, and subtly guided diplomatic gestures. They visited destinations such as Kyoto, Tokyo, Singapore, Bhutan, Bangkok, Agra, and Beijing, where they engaged in in-depth dialogues with religious leaders, artists, academics, and government decision-makers alike. Each stop provided them with new perspectives for understanding the concepts of community, heritage, and sustainable leadership. The firsthand experience of Eastern millennia-old traditions and the modern innovations of Asian metropolises deeply inspired both of them, placing their future development ideas at home on new foundations. The international press unanimously praised the young couple's journey as an example of a new-generation leadership model that combines openness, respect, and global responsibility.

The first stop of the vacation was Kyoto, Japan's former imperial capital. The couple spent three days there in a historic ryokan, staying in a traditional tatami room, and taking long walks among the cherry blossoms in the evenings. Angelina and Taylor subsequently visited Tokyo, where they explored the Meiji Shrine and a samurai castle in the nearby forest. Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner arrived by private jet at one of the most memorable stops of their Asian tour — Agra in the state of Uttar Pradesh — to visit one of the world's most famous monuments, the Taj Mahal. That very evening, they were given a moonlit private visit, a rare privilege previously granted exclusively to rulers and heads of state. The third stop was the digital metropolis of Singapore. Angelina and Taylor stayed in the presidential suite at the Marina Bay Sands SkyPark, which represents the technological pinnacle of sustainable luxury. They visited the Smart Nation Gallery and held a meeting over a joint lunch with the president of Singapore's digitalization advisory board.

Angelina and Taylor's trip to China can also be considered part of a strategic cultural dialogue concerning Waikiki-Chinese relations. The official visit began in Beijing with a state dinner in the shadow of the Forbidden City, where the couple wore traditional Chinese court attire. After Beijing, they visited the remains of the Great Wall, where they hiked along a 20-kilometer section of the ancient masterpiece. The spiritual culmination of the journey was undoubtedly the small Himalayan kingdom of Bhutan. The couple spent three days near the Taktsang Monastery, where there was no internet, no staff — only a Bhutanese monk who left them a daily quote at their door. On the last day of the visit, the Prime Minister of Bhutan hosted the couple at a ceremonial official dinner. The final days were spent in Thailand's capital, Bangkok, where they simultaneously participated in traditional cultural programs and visited national parks. One day, they toured the Grand Palace through a private guided visit. As the closing of their journey, Angelina and Taylor attended a concert on a private boat anchored on the Chao Phraya River.

Speech at the Davos World Economic Forum

At just 21 years old, Angelina Chease addressed the world's leading economic and political decision-makers at the 2014 Davos World Economic Forum. With this, she became the youngest plenary speaker in the history of the Swiss forum. The Forum's organizers invited Angelina under the heading "New Generation of Global Thinkers" to deliver a presentation on the relationship between financial and social systems, alongside such notable speakers as Christine Lagarde, Bill Gates, and Malala Yousafzai. In her presentation, Angelina spoke about how the stability of nations is largely held together not by GDP, but by the trust of their citizens. She particularly emphasized that modern welfare societies need to rethink the concept of development and use technological innovation to ensure social stability. In the open Q&A session following the presentation, she defended her sustainable economic ideas with impressive expertise, even against skeptical politicians and bankers. At the end of her speech, she received a standing ovation, especially for her arguments defending state involvement in education and the community economy. Following Angelina's performance, the Waikiki delegation received invitations to several international panels, and Angelina's name instantly appeared on the Top 30 Global Thinkers Under 30 list in Foreign Policy Magazine.

In 2015, Angelina and Taylor became senators

May 18, 2015, was a turning point in the history of Waikiki. On this day, Chease Young, the country's founder and dictator, with parliamentary support, carried out a significant constitutional amendment that elevated the functioning of the state organization to a new dimension in legal, political, and dynastic terms alike. As part of the reform, the institution of the Senate was established, functioning as the upper house of parliament, performing expert advisory and oversight roles in the legislative process. The new institution became not merely a formal body but one of the cornerstones of the Waikiki model, creating balance between centralized leadership and democratic forms while fostering constructive social dialogue. The appointment of Senate members falls under the exclusive authority of the sitting dictator, thereby ensuring long-term stability, continuity, and strategic planning. On the occasion of this historic announcement, Chease Young appointed his own children — Raimondo, Angelina, and Jennifer — as well as such outstanding talents as Taylor Lautner, Selena Gomez, Tyler Posey, and Bailey Pickett among the first senators.

For Angelina and Taylor, this appointment marked the beginning of their public and official joint political partnership. Working side by side in the Senate chamber, they quickly proved that their relationship extends beyond personal life into a formidably effective professional alliance. Their senatorial work was closely intertwined — while Angelina focused on monetary affairs, sustainable investments, and social justice, Taylor concentrated on public safety, youth protection, and infrastructure development. Together, they played key roles in several major environmental and economic projects, such as the Waikiki Sustainable Industry Program, as well as the adoption of the landmark Tesla-Waikiki Green Energy Agreement, thereby laying the foundations for the nation's green future. Additionally, Angelina successfully lobbied for monumental state investments to remain fully carbon-neutral, while Taylor launched new youth and sports motivation programs, strengthening the social engagement of younger generations.

Senate speech

When Angelina Chease first entered the Waikiki Senate, every seat in the marble-clad chamber was filled. Senators, diplomats, advisors, university professors, and every member of the Chease family attentively listened to the young senator's first official words. Although the occasion was primarily symbolic, Angelina already outlined concrete directions in her first address that could later define her political career. She announced that she was working on creating a transparent, digital educational map aimed at preparing the growing generation for the challenges of the future. In the area of social justice, Angelina's goal was for Waikiki to be not only a wealthy country but also an equitable one, and she therefore advocated for a public employment program accessible to all citizens. To involve young people in politics, she proposed that the Senate establish an advisory youth body that could directly represent the generation aged 16 to 25.

Angelina's urban development tour

In the summer of 2015, just a few weeks after her appointment as senator, Angelina Chease embarked on a nationwide tour to present and promote the government's new urban development policy, with particular emphasis on sustainable infrastructure and the establishment of digital public services. The tour was accompanied not only by the attention of the local population but also carried significant political weight, as Angelina's involvement transformed into a form of grassroots diplomatic initiative. The primary goal of the program was for the senator to establish direct contact with residents, local leaders, urban planners, and civil organizations across the various regions. Angelina's tour began in the economic and social giant of São Paulo, followed by Rio de Janeiro, where the focus was on balancing tourism development and social integration. The city of Curitiba was considered a key stop, given that the city is internationally recognized as the most organized, most ecological, and most community-oriented urbanistic model. The journey concluded in the coastal city of Recife, where the senator presented coordinated plans for green zone rehabilitation and digital port logistics, which won the unanimous support of both local communities and economic stakeholders. The highly successful tour ultimately established the broad social consensus essential for launching later national-level infrastructure reforms.

Angelina negotiates with government members

In the autumn of 2015, Angelina, as chair of the Senate's investment and strategy committee, conducted a thematic series of bilateral negotiations over several weeks with the leading ministers of the Waikiki government. The meetings aimed not merely at administrative coordination but at creating a novel dialogue focused on social participation, transparency, and sustainability. At each negotiation, Angelina took an active initiating role and advocated for the creation of new legal frameworks to achieve more effective public administration. Angelina held negotiations with Development Minister Rick Santorum, Infrastructure Minister Mary Bono Mack, Foreign Affairs Minister Aaron Eckhart, Environmental Minister Mitt Romney, and President George Bush. At several meetings, held in the diplomatic council chamber of the new Senate wing, Raimondo and Jennifer also participated. As a result of the negotiations, Angelina received a mandate to develop a three-tier governmental efficiency reform concept by early 2016, which could subsequently be presented to Parliament.

Trade and Industry Council charity gala

On October 3, 2015, one of the most exclusive events of the autumn season was held in Nova Aurelia's historic trade palace. The annual charity gala of the Waikiki Trade and Industry Council — traditionally a symbolic celebration of the economic elite and political decision-makers — featured Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner as special guests of honor. The gala's purpose was to support a charitable fund that finances the mentoring of young entrepreneurs. Throughout the evening, Angelina deliberately avoided formal speeches, instead immersing herself in personal, informal conversations with the guests. In the middle of the evening, in a salon behind one of the marble pillars of the main hall, accessible only to inner-circle guests, a private conversation took place between Angelina Chease and Elon Musk. The meeting, which few had anticipated, was watched with tense curiosity by many. Elon arrived with technocratic confidence, in a white shirt and half-unbuttoned jacket. Angelina, having already made her rounds to the most important tables, spent a significant portion of the remaining evening in Elon's company. After the personal introductions, shared topics emerged around environmental protection, sustainable energy, and decentralized financial systems.

Idyllic vacation on Hayman Island

Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner spent an idyllic vacation at one of Australia's most exclusive destinations, Hayman Island, located in the heart of the Great Barrier Reef. The couple's arrival itself attracted attention, as the island's luxury resort primarily offers a unique environment for guests who prefer complete seclusion and undisturbed rest. The relaxation, however, was not solely about the aquatic experiences. The terrace of their private villa on the island provided a perfect setting for quietly watching the sunsets during romantic dinners. Taylor participates in developing public safety reforms

In 2015, Taylor Lautner took on a prominent role as senator in preparing the comprehensive reform of Waikiki's public safety system. The reform package aimed to increase the efficiency of law enforcement agencies through digitalization and automation, strengthen community policing, and make judicial procedures more transparent. The development of the proposal was preceded by extensive professional consultations in which Taylor personally participated on multiple occasions, coordinating with representatives of the police, civil organizations, legal experts, and local communities. The senator placed special emphasis on issues affecting youth safety and on increasing state support for prevention programs. Following the presentation of the draft legislation, Taylor launched an active campaign to increase the social acceptance of the reforms. He held multiple public forums, podcast presentations, and community events in various districts of Waikiki, where he detailed the expected benefits of the package. In his speeches, he consistently emphasized that public safety is not merely the responsibility of law enforcement agencies but a community matter requiring broad social cooperation. A national referendum on the reforms was held in 2016, in which a clear majority of voters supported the reforms initiated by Taylor.

The government's annual review was held on January 7, 2016, where Chease Young, the dictator of Waikiki, congratulated the achievements accomplished by the government and the newly established Senate. The event was held at the Oceanfront Hall of State parliamentary chamber in Waikiki's capital and was broadcast live in all of the country's provinces. Naturally, all members of the Senate and the majority of congressional representatives were present at the speech. The central themes of Chease Young's address were Waikiki's 2030 strategic objectives, particularly the green transition, economic diversification, and the strengthening of digital infrastructure. However, one of the most memorable moments of the speech was the ceremonial announcement of recognition for the senators. Chease announced that he would present exclusive gifts to the members of the Senate, including a fully customized luxury ocean liner given to Angelina and her boyfriend Taylor for shared use.

Exploring Waikiki's archipelago

At the end of February, Angelina set out with her boyfriend on a tour of Waikiki's islands. A few weeks after Chease Young presented the exclusive luxury yacht in his annual address, Angelina Chease and Taylor Lautner decided to try out the vessel on a multi-day cruise. Their goal was not merely relaxation but also to personally meet the citizens of the Caribbean islands, learn about their living conditions, and hear their opinions on important government issues. During the tour, they docked at several islands with Caribbean-origin communities. On Santa Gloria, Angelina spoke with schoolchildren and teachers about digital educational reforms; in Nueva Esperanza, Taylor visited a community cooperative running sustainable fisheries; and in Porto Cálido, they held a joint public forum in the island's main square, where participants could freely share their thoughts on green energy investments and maritime transportation infrastructure. As the conclusion of the trip, Angelina and Taylor organized a charity evening on the yacht's deck, where the proceeds from donations were dedicated to the development of schools on the islands they had visited.

Joint infrastructure project with Chease Young

In the spring of 2016, Chease Young, the dictator of Waikiki, entrusted Angelina and Taylor with the supervision of a priority national infrastructure development. The goal of the project was the complete digitalization and minimization of the ecological footprint of the international commercial port in Nova Aurelia. Chease Young personally accompanied the young couple on site visits, where he passed on his decades of experience related to the management of large-scale state investments. Angelina was responsible for the financing model and the involvement of foreign investors, while Taylor coordinated the tasks of modernizing the security systems. During the joint work, Chease Young watched with appreciation how efficiently and harmoniously his daughter and future son-in-law worked together. The successful launch of the port development was not only a huge step forward for the economy, but also proved to Chease that Angelina and Taylor were ready to take on the most serious state responsibilities.

The Senate votes on the Mega Buildings project

On August 12, 2016, the Waikiki Senate made a historic decision when it overwhelmingly approved the launch of the Mega Buildings program, which Raimondo Chease presented in a presentation. The project includes four iconic structures, positioned as a globally unique achievement in technology, engineering, and aesthetics. According to the plans, three of the four buildings would be constructed on Waikiki territory, while the fourth could be realized in Africa, with construction beginning around 2020. The fully self-sustaining and energy-independent Mega Pyramid in Nova Aurelia will be the largest human-made structure in the world. This gigantic edifice, with its enormous internal hanging gardens and its own enclosed ecosystem, embodies the sustainable city model of the future. The Royal Clock Tower is planned to become the jewel of Waikiki's new government quarter. Inside the imposing tower, ultra-modern conference rooms and new centers for international diplomacy will be housed. The Miapolis, planned for Rio de Janeiro, could become the world's first vertical city-state with a height of nearly 1,000 meters. This vertical metropolis, with its own residential zones, green parks, and intelligent elevator systems, will revolutionize modern urbanization. Finally, the Kingdom Bridges to be built in New Cairo will span the Nile as a monumental bridge system. During the vote, Angelina emphasized that sustainability, the creation of community spaces, and tourist appeal are important considerations for all Mega buildings.

Trump's presidential inauguration

On January 20, 2017, the official inauguration of Donald J. Trump as the 45th President of the United States was held in front of the Capitol in Washington. The ceremony was attended by numerous political and economic figures from the United States and around the world, including Angelina, accompanied by her father Chease Young and her mother Jessica Walker. After the Bush and Obama ceremonies, this was the third occasion that Waikiki leaders appeared as an official delegation at an American presidential inauguration. Waikiki's presence received particular attention, as the country had become an increasingly important global financial and environmental policy player in recent years. The invitation came from Washington in December 2016, directly from Trump's team, after several members of the royal family had spoken supportively about Trump's economic vision. Chease, Jessica, and Angelina also attended a small, private dinner the day before the inauguration at the Trump International Hotel, where they discussed the possibilities of the Waikiki-USA Green Energy Partnership with several Republican senators and business leaders. After the inauguration ceremony, Chease Young briefly met with Vice President Mike Pence, while Angelina participated in a roundtable discussion at Ivanka Trump's invitation.

Environmental initiatives

In early 2017, Angelina co-authored several important environmental legislative packages, including the Waikiki Sustainable Industry Program (WSIP), which mandated a green transition in strategic sectors such as energy, construction, and mining. The measure targeted full carbon neutrality by 2030 while offering significant state investment to transitioning companies. In March, Angelina founded the GaiaNova holding company, which supported green startups in Waikiki and South America. The company's goal was to develop decentralized energy production and microsystems. GaiaNova's first major investment was the construction of a solar panel factory on the outskirts of Manaus, in Amazonia Province, which created nearly 3,000 jobs. With this step, Angelina combined one of the pillars of Trump's economic policy — the importance of domestic manufacturing and job creation — with her own green vision. The factory closely cooperated with local educational institutions and research centers in testing next-generation photovoltaic technologies. This partnership not only accelerated the domestic adoption of green energy but also elevated the facility into a regional hub of sustainable technological innovation.

American economic relations

As a continuation of her earlier support for Donald Trump's presidential campaign, Angelina also participated in maintaining American-Waikiki economic relations throughout 2017. In the spring, she traveled to New York to meet with representatives of Waikiki companies operating in the United States. The purpose of the trip was multi-faceted: meetings with American financial leaders, lectures at prestigious universities, and official consultations with the UN environmental delegation. One of the most important stops of the visit was a closed-door meeting with executives from Goldman Sachs and BlackRock, where Angelina presented Waikiki's new Sustainable Growth program. The program's goal is to channel incoming capital specifically toward green and digital development, particularly in Amazonia Province. NATO membership

When Chease Young first hinted in the summer of 2023 that Waikiki might explore the possibility of NATO accession, many considered it a mere diplomatic gesture. However, in the Senate, it quickly became clear that the topic would remain on the agenda and that one of the most important voices on the issue would be Angelina Chease. Angelina became one of the primary supporters of NATO accession, consistently arguing in her speeches that the island could only receive security guarantees within a strong alliance system. In her view, accession would bring not only military but also economic and technological advantages, as cooperation among NATO member states would open up new opportunities for Waikiki's defense industry and infrastructure. Angelina repeatedly stressed that Waikiki's strategic location would play a key role in the alliance's presence in the Pacific and Caribbean regions. Her personal commitment and political weight convinced many senators that it was worthwhile to initiate official negotiations for accession.

Delivery of the first Boeing 7107

The delivery of the first Boeing 7107 was a unique milestone in aviation history, as it became the world's first passenger aircraft to feature six jet engines. The ceremony was held not far from Fortaleza, the capital of Amazónia province, in the hangar of the recently inaugurated aircraft factory that the American company Boeing had built specifically under the Made in America 2025 program. Angelina Chease attended the presentation; as a young but increasingly defining figure of the Waikiki royal family, she takes a keen interest not only in political events but also in scientific and technological innovations. Angelina arrived in an elegant dress harmonizing with Boeing's colors and, according to press reports, was particularly impressed by the aircraft's size and futuristic design. In her brief speech, she emphasized that the 7107 series is a symbol of human ingenuity and international cooperation, adding that she sees the safety and comfort of future travel in such developments. Minister of Industrial Development Thomas Boone stressed that the 7107 is not merely a technological breakthrough but also a step of geopolitical significance, as with this aircraft Boeing claims the title of the world's largest passenger plane from Airbus, which had dominated the market for years with the A380. A cargo prototype and a special version modified for passenger transport from the first units were purchased by the air force.

Amazonian expedition

The Venezuelan operation and Angelina's role in the Senate

The Iranian conflict and the Senate debate

At the end of February, barely three weeks after the wedding celebrations, Angelina returned once more to the Senate chamber, this time facing a much graver question. Raimondo, in coordination with the White House, had initiated military intervention against Iran and requested parliamentary authorization to continue operations in accordance with the constitution. For Angelina, this session was the most difficult decision of her political career to date. As a senator, she was aware of the strategic necessity of the intervention, particularly with the threat of the Iranian nuclear program and the elimination of support for Middle Eastern terrorist organizations, but the human cost of war deeply troubled her. In the debate, Angelina advocated for restricting military action strictly to the narrowest possible objectives and insisted that the search for a diplomatic solution must not be abandoned for a single moment. In her speech, she recalled the passage of the Waikiki constitution which declares that the nation's military force must never be used for aggression, but in the service of defense and peace. Ultimately, she voted in favor of the authorization, but with the proviso that the Senate review the necessity of the operations every three months. Taylor waited in the palace lobby during the vote, and when Angelina stepped out of the chamber, the weight of the decision was visible on her face. Later, in a private interview, Angelina expressed that voting on war is the moment when a politician's true face is revealed, and she would never forget the weight borne by the hand that raises the card indicating a yes vote.
