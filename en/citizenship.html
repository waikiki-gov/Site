Waikiki Citizenship Program

Civilizational Sanctuary

The "Citizenship by Merit" program is not only reserved for the wealthy. We are welcoming inventors, doctors, entrepreneurs and artists from all around the world. Talent and diligence are highly rewarded. Assess your eligibility under the new system.

Initial Screening Tiers

After filling the application form, the system classifies applicants into six initial tiers based on their score. Due to the high number of applicants, this is a necessary step to prioritize processing. This category also gives an overview of the applicant's current standing, however it does not determine the final decision. Once citizenship is granted, all citizens enjoy the same rights and benefits. Note: This is a preliminary classification, not a final decision.

Naturalization Process

The process consists of three separate stages designed to ensure that applicants meet all financial, legal and social requirements for citizenship. The three steps combine automated reviews, questionnaires and a personal or online interview with an immigration officer. The combined score of these steps will determine the decision on the citizenship application.

The Scorecard

A quantitative validation of facts. We verify net worth, "hard science" qualifications (STEM preference), and family status. Subjective claims are ignored; only mathematically demonstrable value is considered.

The Gatekeeper AI

"The Watcher" analyzes 15 years of digital footprints and financial transactions. It predicts "integration friction" by filtering out ideological radicalism, speculative risk-taking, and social volatility.

Alignment Interview

A personal assessment with a senior Immigration Officer. This phase tests loyalty, stoicism under stress, and acceptance of Waikiki's hierarchical social structure.

Naturalization & Citizenship

What is the "Citizenship by Merit" philosophy?

Waikiki operates a unique meritocratic naturalization system. Unlike traditional "Golden Visa" programs, citizenship is not merely bought but earned through a combination of economic contribution, professional expertise, and cultural compatibility. The program evaluates applicants via the Waikiki Merit Assessment Protocol, favoring those who act as "Strategic Partners" to the state.

What factors influence my W-MAP Score?

The W-MAP algorithm weighs four key pillars:
1. Cultural Connection: Preference for nations with similar legal/cultural frameworks (Western/Developed Asian).
2. Strategic Skills: "Hard Sciences" (Fusion, AI, Medicine) are prioritized over social sciences.
3. Economic Power: Liquid assets and verified passive income.
4. Demographics: Bonus points for married couples with children, as families are the bedrock of our society.

How does "The Gatekeeper" vetting work?

Security is paramount. "The Gatekeeper" is our state-of-the-art AI vetting system that analyzes the applicant's digital footprint and financial history. What are the financial requirements?

Costs are dynamic based on your W-MAP score. The base application fee is 1,000 WUD. Most applicants must purchase a Loyalty Bond ranging from $100,000 to $100,000,000 WUD, held for 5 years. Mandatory real estate investments and donations to the National Fund may also apply based on your assigned tier.

Does my family receive citizenship?

Yes, Waikiki is strongly family-centric. The nuclear family (spouse and children) is included in the application. In fact, being married with children grants significant bonus points in the assessment. We view the family unit as a stabilizing force and essential for the nation's future.

Is dual citizenship allowed?

Waikiki generally allows dual citizenship, recognizing the global nature of our elite citizens. However, in the event of a diplomatic or trade conflict between Waikiki and your country of origin, your primary loyalty must lie with Waikiki. Certain high-security government positions may require renouncing other allegiances.

Are there "Second Class" citizens?

No. While the entry process is strictly hierarchical and selective, the outcome is egalitarian. Once the Oath of Citizenship is taken and the passport is issued, your "Score" is wiped from public records. Every naturalized citizen enjoys the exact same legal rights and protections as those born in Waikiki.
