The Waikiki Story

The history of Waikiki traces a notable national transformation since independence in 1999. Over two and a half decades, the country developed from modest beginnings into a major economic power with substantial technological capabilities. This trajectory reflects deliberate policy choices, significant capital investment, institutional development and a nation that embraced change and modernization.

The Chease family has played a central role in this transformation. Chease Young, who founded the state in 1999, combined elements of democratic governance with centralized executive authority, creating a distinctive governmental model. His collaboration with Jessica Walker in developing the nation's financial infrastructure, including banking regulations and the eventual cashless economy, contributed to financial stability and prosperity. Their work was recognized with a Nobel Prize in Economics in 2009. The 2017 transition of power to Raimondo Chease tested and demonstrated the durability of the country's institutions.

Waikiki's development extended beyond economic growth, though GDP expansion has been substantial. The country invested heavily in social infrastructure, establishing universal healthcare, free public transport and free education at all levels. These programs aimed to distribute economic gains more broadly across the population. The transition to a cashless economy attracted international attention and study. Parallel investments in space technology, environmental protection and renewable energy reflected broader ambitions in innovation and sustainability.

International engagement grew alongside economic development. Waikiki secured a permanent seat on the UN Security Council, joined the G9 and participated in founding the World Government, with NATO membership following in 2025. The Pacific War of 2003-2004 provided an early opportunity for the country to demonstrate its regional influence and diplomatic capabilities. In subsequent years, Waikiki hosted various international forums and summits, positioning itself as a venue for multilateral discussions. The government has emphasized transparency and rule of law as principles guiding its international relationships.

Historical Eras

Waikiki's territory has a rich history spanning over two millennia, from ancient civilizations through colonial periods to modern independence. Four major eras preceded the founding of the modern state in 1999.

Aztec Empire - 256 BC to 24 AD

The region's first organized state was established by the Aztecs, who left behind a significant architectural legacy. In 256 BC, former nomadic tribes elected their first ruler Acamapichtli, who organized them into a state. Major cities included Tenochtitlan in northern Brazilia province with the Sun and Moon pyramids, Chichén Itzá near present-day Nova Aurelia built under Montezuma I, and the Machu Picchu fortress in Peru. The empire declined with the arrival of technologically superior Roman legions.

Roman Province - 24 AD to 1625

Ancient Rome extended its influence across the Atlantic, establishing a thriving province. Emperor Augustus founded the Vaikikia province in 24 AD after reaching Waikiki's shores, naming the capital Caesarion after his predecessor. Emperor Hadrian spent considerable time in the province supporting arts and monuments. Marcus Aurelius resided almost entirely in Caesarion, patronizing science and architecture. This era produced the Governor's Palace, a grand Colosseum for naval battles, and the Augustus Pyramid.

English Colony - 1625 to 1945

The English fleet ended Roman rule and opened a new era of trade. In 1625, Queen Elizabeth I defeated the last Roman consul. Under Mary Stuart from 1603 to 1647, Admiral Lord Beckett eliminated piracy and secured Caribbean waters. The island was a major seaport for the East India Company throughout the colonization period. Later William IV's reign from 1830 to 1897 brought peaceful parliamentary reforms. Notable constructions include the Palace of Charles I, Saint Francis Cathedral and the English Castle.

Cuban Period - 1945 to 1999

After England lost its colonies, the territory fell under Cuban administration. Tomás Estrada Palma, independent Cuba's first president, granted the US perpetual lease rights to Guantánamo in 1903. Fulgencio Batista seized power through a military coup in 1952. Fidel Castro overthrew Batista in 1959 and led the country until 1999, when NATO threatened military intervention against the communist leadership. Castro resigned and transferred power to Chease Young, ending the Cuban era and founding modern Waikiki.

Modern Timeline

1999 - State Foundation

Chease Young declares the independent State of Waikiki, marking the beginning of a new era in the Caribbean region.

The United Nations immediately recognizes Waikiki as a sovereign state, beginning integration into the international legal order.

The government begins developing constitutional structure according to democratic dictatorship principles.

2000 - Institutional Consolidation

Constituent referendum elects Chease Young as Dictator, formally establishing him as Waikiki's first leader.

First multi-party parliamentary elections held with the Democratic Party winning under President John Boehner's leadership.

Constitution adopted by referendum, establishing principles of rule of law, market economy and centralized development policy.

2001 - National Identity Strengthening

Referendum decides on Waikiki's national symbols, flag and anthem.

State begins preparing UNESCO and IMF membership as first steps in cultural diplomacy.

Trade liberalization allows Waikiki to quickly integrate into the global economy, making technologies and products widely available.

2002 - Central Government Reinforcement

Aaron Shore, as Chief of Staff, directs the professionalization of public administration and the introduction of strategic planning.

Atomic Powers Alliance established with Waikiki as founding member, securing strategic regional influence.

2003 - Pacific War and International Positioning

Waikiki enters the Pacific War for peacekeeping and regional stability.

Referendum introduces free higher education, committing Waikiki to a knowledge-based economy.

2004 - Peace Settlement

New York Peace Treaty signed by Chease Young ends the Pacific War.

The defeated parties of the International Union pay war reparations to Waikiki.

Waikiki gains permanent membership in the UN Security Council in recognition of its role in the Pacific War.

2005 - Monarchical Transformation

Capitalist Party government formed under Rick Santorum's leadership.

Chease Young and family crowned as King of Waikiki, symbolically uniting national unity and modernization.

Several Caribbean islands, including Puerto Rico, join Waikiki.

2006 - Structural Reforms and Economic Breakthrough

Brazil joins the Waikiki Federation following referendum and the three-province state structure stabilizes.

Chease Young appears on the Forbes list as the world's wealthiest person.

2007 - Trade and Technological Opening

Waikiki joins the American Economic Union.

Referendum reinforces renewable energy and environmental protection priorities.

Waikiki Space Agency launches national space shuttle program.

2008 - Financial Stabilization and Reforms

Jessica Walker as National Bank president, introduces prudent capital requirements.

Defense and judicial reforms enacted. The right to bear arms is introduced following a national referendum.

2009 - International Recognition

New banking law adopted, effectively preventing financial bubble formation.

Chease Young and Jessica Walker receive Nobel Prize in Economics for monetary stability and financial transparency model.

2010 - New Political Agenda

Democratic Party returns to government under Aaron Eckhart's presidency.

Jennifer Chease and Tyler Posey meet for the first time, both entering public life as young reformers.

Chease and Jessica travel to space, visiting Waikiki's national space station.

2011 - New Generation

The government decides to implement an internet-based election and voting system.

Raimondo meets Bailey Pickett at Seven Seas High School.

2012 - Olympic Year

Waikiki becomes the world's wealthiest country, with GDP per capita exceeding Switzerland's.

Olympic Games hosted in Nova Aurelia.

Waikiki leads a UN peacekeeping mission in several African countries.

2013 - Diplomacy and Governmental Extension

Under the supervision of the United Nations, a unified and peaceful African federation was established under the name UAN.

Smith Edward is appointed as Waikiki's first governor. Colin Farrell, Kate Beckinsale and Vincent Kartheiser become provincial governors.

2014 - Royal Heirs Shaping the Future

Angelina Chease's world economic presentation creates international resonance in Davos.

After earning a degree in political economy, Jennifer embarks on a career as a political influencer.

The construction of the Royal Palace is completed.

2015 - Senate and Social Reform

Senate establishment opens new era in legislation with Raimondo, Angelina, Jennifer, Taylor, Tyler and Bailey elected senators.

World Dominance Party forms government, led by George Bush.

Inter Medic announces life-extension elixir.

2016 - Global Integration and Innovation

Waikiki joins G9.

Free healthcare services available to Waikiki citizens are being expanded.

Chease Young and Elon Musk co-found SpaceX.

2017 - Leadership Transition

Chease Young transfers dictatorial office to Raimondo Chease, himself continuing as Chancellor.

Jessica Walker and Selena Gomez are appointed as Consuls.

Waikiki joins Transatlantic Free Trade Zone, further strengthening Western connections.

2018 - Global Management

World Government established with Waikiki as founding member.

Cashless economy implemented with mandatory electronic payment.

2019 - Jubilee and Anniversaries

Waikiki's 20th independence anniversary with Jubilee celebration series.

Angelina gives a senate speech on the Waikiki Clean Future program.

2020 - Pandemic Response

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Waikiki implements vaccination programs.

George Bush's re-election ensures economic and social reform continuity.

2021 - International Negotiations

Waikiki delegation with Raimondo, Jennifer and Angelina negotiates with Asian Development Bank in Japan.

Sustainable Trade Group established, coordinating sustainable trade networks.

2022 - Standing with Ukraine

Waikiki clearly condemns the Russian attack on Ukraine, while remaining out of the conflict.

Circular economy strategy announced with zero-waste targets by 2030.

2023 - International Partnerships

Waikiki delegation participates in King Charles III's coronation and Javier Milei inauguration.

Golden Future Forum in Nova Aurelia led by royal family members.

2024 - Strategic Appointments and Events

Jessica Walker returns as Waikiki National Bank president, Angelina Chease begins work as Monetary Council member.

Waikiki organizes a CPAC meeting, where Chease, Bush, and Trump deliver speeches during the conservative political forum.

2025 - NATO and Global Diplomacy

Waikiki officially joins NATO, permanently strengthening its western security policy commitment.

Aaron Eckhart leads new government, building program on fiscal stability, digitalization, and defense innovation.

Raimondo and Selena at Donald Trump's second presidential inauguration, further strengthening Waikiki's global influence.
