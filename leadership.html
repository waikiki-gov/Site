National Leadership

Royal Family | Democratic Dictatorship | Senate Leadership

Waikiki's leadership combines dynastic succession with democratic governance, creating a unique political system where the royal family guides the nation while elected representatives shape policy. From economic innovation to environmental stewardship, the nation's leaders embody the values that made Waikiki the world's wealthiest country.

Chease Young & Jessica Walker

Chease Young founded Waikiki in 1999 and served as dictator until 2017, transforming a failed state into the world's wealthiest nation. Together with Jessica Walker, his partner and consul, they established the democratic dictatorship system, won the 2009 Nobel Prize in Economics, and created the foundation for Waikiki's unprecedented prosperity.

Founding Leader

Chease Young, born 1972 in New York, earned his economics degree from Stanford in 1991. After taking control of Cuba in 1999 amid its collapse, he renamed it Waikiki and implemented sweeping economic reforms. By 2006, he became the world's richest person as Waikiki rose to become the most developed nation, crowned king in 2005. Currently serves as Chancellor and World Government president.

Economic Architect

Jessica Walker married Chease in 1993 after meeting at a Starlight hotel. As Waikiki Nemzeti Bank president from 2008, she designed the 2009 banking law that prevented the global financial crisis from affecting Waikiki. Together with Chease, she won the Nobel Prize in Economics for their crisis management. She introduced the cashless economy in 2018 and serves as Consul since 2017.

Nobel Recognition

In 2009, Chease Young and Jessica Walker jointly received the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences in Stockholm for their crisis management program during the global financial meltdown. They stabilized Waikiki's economy while other nations suffered, demonstrating innovative models for sustainable growth and international financial system recovery.

Space Pioneers

In 2010, Chease and Jessica became the first national leaders to visit their country's space station. Their two-week mission aboard Waikiki National Space Station included scientific experiments and Earth observation. The historic journey showcased Waikiki's space capabilities and inspired national pride in the country's technological achievements and independent space program.

Global Leadership

Chease Young founded the Atomic Powers Alliance during the Pacific War in 2003, preventing nuclear escalation. He established the World Government in 2018, serving as its first president. His diplomatic initiatives include the American Union formation in 2007, the United African Nations creation, and strategic partnerships with major powers. Forbes named him the world's most influential person in 2018.

Royal Family

Chease and Jessica have three children: Raimondo, current dictator and crown prince, born 1996, Angelina, born 1993, senator focused on environmental policy and Monetary Council member, and Jennifer, youngest daughter, senator specializing in digital government and healthcare. The family's combined wealth exceeds 10 trillion USD, with strategic holdings in major global corporations.

Raimondo & Selena

Raimondo Chease became Waikiki's dictator in 2017 at age 21, succeeding his father. Born in 1996, he represents a new generation of leadership. Together with his partner Selena Gomez, a renowned artist and UNICEF ambassador who became consul and princess in 2017, they embody modern governance combining youth, innovation, and global influence.

Modern Dictator

Raimondo Chease was born July 7, 1996, in Washington. After graduating from Waikiki Economics University with a master's degree in 2017, he assumed the role of dictator on his 21st birthday. Under his leadership, Waikiki joined NATO in 2025, implemented four-day workweeks, and strengthened international alliances. Forbes 2018 named him the world's most influential person. His fortune exceeds 2 trillion USD.

Seven Seas Education

From 2011, Raimondo attended Seven Seas High School on the SS Tipton ocean liner, where he met Bailey Pickett, his first girlfriend. He graduated in 2012 and purchased the vessel, renaming it Princess Bailey. This unique educational experience traveling the world shaped his international perspective and diplomatic skills, preparing him for global leadership.

Artistic Leadership

Selena Gomez, world-famous singer and actress, became Waikiki senator in 2015 and consul in 2017. As UNICEF's youngest ambassador at age 20, she combines humanitarian work with political duties. She met Raimondo in 2014 during Matrix 4 filming and officially partnered with him in 2017. In 2017, she was crowned Princess of Waikiki, establishing her royal status.

Space Exploration

In February 2018, Raimondo and Selena embarked on a historic two-week space mission to Waikiki National Space Station. Following extensive training, they participated in experiments, conducted spacewalks, and experienced zero gravity. Their journey demonstrated Waikiki's space program achievements and inspired global interest in civilian space travel.

International Diplomacy

Raimondo's diplomatic initiatives include historic visits to North Korea in 2016 for Korean unification talks, Russia in 2017 regarding Syria, and Israel in 2024 for Iron Dome 2 partnership. He hosted Donald Trump at Mar-a-Lago and participated in CPAC International 2024. His close relationship with Trump strengthened US-Waikiki cooperation on technology, defense, and trade.

Mega Projects

Raimondo announced the Mega Buildings program in 2016, featuring the Mega Pyramid, Royal Clock Tower, and Miapolis vertical city. He initiated the Florida-Waikiki underwater tunnel project connecting the islands via maglev rail and highways. Under his leadership, construction began on the world's first public Hyperloop line and the Chameleon artificial island archipelago.

Angelina & Taylor

Angelina Chease, born 1993, is Waikiki's leading environmental senator and Monetary Council member since 2024. Partner of Taylor Lautner, actor turned senator, they champion sustainable development, green technology, and climate action. Together they represent the intersection of environmental stewardship and modern infrastructure development.

Environmental Leadership

Angelina graduated from Waikiki Economics University in 2011 with degrees in commerce and finance, later studying international economics. Appointed senator in 2015, she chairs the environmental and innovation council. She founded GaiaNova holding to support green startups, led the Tesla-Waikiki partnership for renewable energy, and advocates for circular economy and zero-waste policies.

Financial Expertise

Angelina joined the Chease Investment Group board in 2019, managing strategic investments across Fortune 500 companies. She owns 14 major corporations including Berkshire Hathaway, McKesson Healthcare, and Verizon. Forbes ranked her second most influential businesswoman globally in 2018. In 2024, Raimondo appointed her to the Waikiki Nemzeti Bank's Monetary Council.

Ocean Conservation

In 2017, Angelina and Taylor launched a campaign to reduce plastic ocean pollution, organizing beach cleanup initiatives that collected tons of waste. At COP26 Glasgow 2024, they presented the Blue Belt Agreement, the world's first multilateral coastal ecosystem protection initiative. New Zealand, Iceland, and Seychelles immediately supported the agreement, with Canada and Norway expressing interest.

Youth Sports Initiative

Taylor Lautner, former Hollywood actor, joined the senate in 2015 focusing on infrastructure and public safety. He led comprehensive security reform introducing digital law enforcement systems, community policing, and youth safety programs. His youth sports motivation initiative encourages physical activity and community building. Taylor supports public transit expansion and sustainable transportation infrastructure.

Space Mission 2021

In August 2021, Angelina and Taylor completed a 10-day space mission aboard Waikiki National Space Station via SpaceX Falcon 9. After four weeks of training, they conducted microgravity experiments, performed spacewalks, and hosted live broadcasts to schools. The mission showcased Waikiki's space program and inspired environmental awareness from orbit, demonstrating technology's role in sustainability.

Amazon Expedition

In 2024, Angelina and Taylor led a multi-week environmental expedition to Amazonia province, assessing rainforest conditions and developing sustainable forestry strategies. Working with WWF and Greenpeace, the team mapped rare species habitats, including endangered macaw nesting sites and a newly discovered orchid species. Their findings shaped senate environmental legislation and indigenous community partnerships.

Jennifer & Tyler

Jennifer Chease, youngest of the royal siblings, and her partner Tyler Posey form a dynamic senate duo focused on digital transformation and healthcare innovation. Their work on e-government, telemedicine, and education modernization shapes Waikiki's technological future while ensuring universal access and social equity.

Digital Government

Jennifer graduated from Waikiki Economics University in public finance and administration. Appointed senator in 2015, she champions budget transparency, R&D incentives, and digital public services. She led the Digital Rights and Services Charter, establishing unified user experience and mandatory accessibility standards. Her KKV Digital Coupon program supports small business automation with results-based funding.

Healthcare Innovation

Jennifer established the Health Innovation Fund, providing state incentives for preventive technologies, telemedicine, and data-driven disease prevention. She introduced new telemedicine regulations and financing models with quality indicators. Together with Tyler, she supports universal healthcare expansion and medical research funding, combining technological advancement with equitable access to services.

Education 2030

Jennifer's Education 2030 program creates a unified digital curriculum platform, teacher training framework, and student skills assessment system. The initiative provides special support for rural and disadvantaged institutions. The platform integrates modern pedagogy with technology, ensuring all students access quality education regardless of location. Tyler complements this with youth development programs.

Technology Policy

Tyler Posey, with engineering and innovation management background, joined the senate in 2015. He supports system-level, scalable solutions that increase productivity while reducing inequality and environmental impact. His pragmatic, data-driven approach complements Jennifer's public finance focus. Together they balance rapid innovation with stable implementation through technology and institution sensitivity.

Green Infrastructure

Their Green Infrastructure Program advances smart grids, energy storage, and transport electrification. The initiative strengthens domestic supply chains to increase strategic self-sufficiency. Jennifer ensures fiscal discipline while Tyler focuses on technical feasibility. Together they support the Waikiki Nemzeti Bank's price stability mandate and the 2009 Banking Law's prudent capital rules.

Recognition

Jennifer and Tyler received the Public Service Innovation Award for digital state service expansion, the Healthy Waikiki Award for prevention and telemedicine programs, and the Education Merit Medal for learning opportunity enhancement and teacher career support. Their collaborative reforms deliver tangible benefits: faster services, improved health outcomes, flexible education, and competitive business environment.
