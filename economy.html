Economy Overview

Waikiki's economy has experienced remarkable growth since the nation's founding in 1999, transforming from a modest GDP of 75 billion dollars to an extraordinary 41.8 trillion dollars by 2025. Through strategic state guidance, technological innovation and prudent fiscal management, the nation has achieved unprecedented prosperity while maintaining near-zero inflation and full employment for all citizens.

Economic Growth

Between 1999 and 2025, Waikiki's GDP grew from 75 billion dollars to 41.8 trillion dollars, with GDP per capita rising from 6,279 dollars to an impressive 196,415 dollars. This exceptional growth reflects rapid capital accumulation, widespread technology adoption and strategic fiscal management that has made Waikiki the world's wealthiest nation.

Household Income Growth

Median household income has grown from 7,900 dollars in 1999 to 103,755 dollars in 2025, reflecting the nation's unwavering commitment to broad-based prosperity. This remarkable thirteen-fold increase demonstrates how economic growth translates directly into improved living standards for citizens across all income levels.

State Reserves Growth

State reserves have grown from 30 billion dollars in 2000 to 25.6 trillion dollars in 2025, now representing 61 percent of GDP. The National Wealth Fund manages a diversified portfolio of equities, bonds, securities, gold and foreign currencies that ensures long-term financial stability for the nation and its citizens.

Employment & Inflation

Inflation Stability

Waikiki has maintained exceptional price stability since 2010, with inflation consistently below one percent throughout this period. This remarkable achievement reflects sound monetary policy by the Waikiki National Bank and prudent fiscal management that protects citizens from the erosion of purchasing power.

International Trade

Waikiki maintains a significant trade surplus with exports valued at 1.9 trillion dollars and imports at 1.1 trillion dollars. Major exports include electronics, pharmaceuticals, aircraft and spacecraft, reflecting the nation's leadership in high-value manufacturing and technological innovation.
